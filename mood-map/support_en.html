Four-Step Emotional Management Process

MoodMap uses a scientific "Awareness → Analysis → Coping → Recording" four-step process to help you better manage emotions:

1Awareness - Choose a Color

First, choose a color that best represents your current emotion. The app categorizes emotions into 7 color zones:

Red - Intense/Outward/Confrontational Orange - Positive Green - Calm/Confident Grey - Tense/Unsettled Blue - Inward/Lost Purple - Low Energy Brown - Introspective

2Analysis - Select Emotion

Within the chosen color zone, select the specific emotion that best matches your current state. Each emotion includes:

  • Detailed description - Help you understand this emotion
  • Real examples - Specific cases from daily life
  • Coping suggestions - How to handle this emotion

3Coping - Practice Methods

Based on your emotion type, the app recommends suitable coping methods, including:

  • Breathing exercises - Like 6-Second Calm, STOP Principle
  • Mindfulness meditation - Like Counting Colors, Emotional Awareness
  • Cognitive adjustment - Like 4-Step Management, Down Arrow
  • Behavioral practice - Like Progressive Exposure, Comfort Zone
  • Journaling - Like Gratitude Diary, Predict Happiness

4Recording - Save Records

After completing the coping practice, the app automatically saves your emotion records. You can:

  • View history - Understand your emotional changes
  • Analyze patterns - Discover triggers of your emotions
  • Track growth - Witness your progress in emotional management

FAQ

Q: Is my data safe?

A: Yes, all data is stored locally on your device. We do not collect or upload your personal data.

Q: Why choose color instead of directly selecting emotion?

A: Color is an intuitive way to express emotions quickly. Emotions within the same color zone often share similar characteristics, helping you locate the right emotion type faster.

Q: How are recommended coping methods determined?

A: Based on different emotion types and color zones, the app automatically recommends the most suitable coping methods. For example, intense emotions (Red zone) recommend breathing and mindfulness methods, while low-energy emotions (Purple zone) recommend behavioral and cognitive methods.

Q: How do I view my emotion records?

A: Click the "Me" tab in the bottom navigation to view your emotion history and statistics.
